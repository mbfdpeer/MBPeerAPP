When to reach out

You do not need to be falling apart to reach out.

This is for the calls that stay in your head, the ones that hit different, or the stress that keeps building after the shift ends.

  • ●Calls involving children or severe injury
  • ●Line of duty death, suicide, or serious injury
  • ●Incidents involving someone you know
  • ●Any call that feels off and won't leave you alone
Trust & confidentiality

Yes — you can confide in trained peers from your own department.

This support model is built around responders speaking with responders who understand the job, the culture, and what a bad call can do after the sirens stop.

Talking to CISM is not the same as reporting to the department.
CISM Peer Support Team

Reach out to anyone on this team.

Every member is trained to listen with full discretion. They will hear you out and connect you to the right resource — one-on-one, chaplain, clinician, debriefing, or whatever fits best.

What happens next

Simple. Quiet. No drama.

  • 1A trained peer listens — no agenda, no report, no pressure.
  • 2Together you figure out what kind of support fits: one-on-one, small group, chaplain, clinician.
  • 3The peer connects you to that resource — you do not have to navigate it alone.
Available One-on-one, defusing, debriefing, follow-up, referral
Important No judgment. No pressure. Participation is voluntary.
Confidentiality

What you say stays protected.

  • ✓Not part of discipline
  • ✓Not a performance review
  • ✓Not a quality assurance meeting
  • ✓Protected within the peer support setting

Confidentiality only breaks in a few situations.

  • !Risk of harm to yourself
  • !Risk of harm to someone else
  • !Suspected child abuse or elder abuse
Watch for signs

Reach out early.

You do not need to wait until things are bad. Early support is usually the strongest move.

  • ●Trouble sleeping or shutting your mind off
  • ●Replaying the call or getting stuck on details
  • ●Feeling numb, detached, angry, or short-fused
  • ●Drinking more or isolating more than usual
